DEEP DIVE

The Complete History

A40

Celica Supra (A40) - 1978-1981

The original Supra was born as a longer, more powerful version of the Celica. Featuring a 2.6L inline-6 engine producing 110 HP, it marked the beginning of Toyota's sports car legacy. The A40 introduced the world to what would become one of the most iconic sports car nameplates.

  • Engine: 2.6L Inline-6 (4M-E)
  • Power: 110 HP
  • 0-60: 10.2 seconds
  • Production: 1978-1981
A60

Celica Supra (A60) - 1981-1986

The second generation brought significant improvements in power and handling. With a new 2.8L engine and available digital dashboard, the A60 showed Toyota's commitment to technological leadership in the sports car segment.

  • Engine: 2.8L Inline-6 (5M-GE)
  • Power: 145 HP
  • 0-60: 8.4 seconds
  • Production: 1981-1986
A70

Supra (A70) - 1986-1993

Supra finally became its own model, separate from Celica. The A70 introduced turbocharging with the 7M-GTE engine, producing 230 HP. This generation established the Supra as a serious performance contender.

  • Engine: 3.0L Inline-6 Turbo (7M-GTE)
  • Power: 230 HP
  • 0-60: 6.1 seconds
  • Production: 1986-1993
A80

Supra (A80) - 1993-2002

The legendary MK4 Supra with the iconic 2JZ-GTE twin-turbo engine. Capable of supporting over 1,000 HP with minimal modifications, it became a tuner's dream and cultural icon through the Fast & Furious franchise.

  • Engine: 3.0L Twin-Turbo Inline-6 (2JZ-GTE)
  • Power: 320 HP (Japan: 276 HP)
  • 0-60: 4.6 seconds
  • Production: 1993-2002
A90

GR Supra (A90) - 2019-Present

After a 17-year hiatus, the Supra returns. Developed in collaboration with BMW and tuned by Gazoo Racing, the A90 combines modern technology with the spirit of its predecessors. The B58 engine provides exhilarating performance.

  • Engine: 3.0L Turbo Inline-6 (B58)
  • Power: 382 HP
  • 0-60: 3.9 seconds
  • Production: 2019-Present
